Your blood donation can help save lives



WHY WE NEED DONORS


  1. There is no substitute for blood. The community supply depends on people like you.
  2. The blood supply needs to be replenished constantly. Blood products have a short shelf life, from 5 to 42 days.
  3. Right now, not enough people donate blood. Only 4 people out of 100 who are able to donate blood do so.
  4. Blood is in constant demand for accident victims, surgery and cancer patients and more.

WHAT TO DO BEFORE DONATING


  1. To donate blood, you must be in good health, 16 years of age (16-year-olds need a completed and signed Parental Consent Form) or older, and weigh a minimum of 110 lbs.
  2. Whole blood donation process takes about 10-15 minutes. Automated procedures can take anywhere from 45 minutes to 90 minutes.
  3. Eat a light healthy meal. Avoid fatty foods. Donating blood on an empty stomach is not recommended. Eat iron rich foods a few days leading up to your blood donation.
  4. Get a good night sleep before the night before your donation and drink plenty of water prior to donating.
  5. On your blood donation day, wear comfortable clothing, preferably with a short-sleeve shirt. Bring your ID and a list of any medications you are taking. If you are 16, you'll need a signed parental consent form.

WHAT TO DO AFTER DONATING


  1. Don't do any heavy lifting or vigorous exercise for the rest of the day.
  2. Keep the strip bandage on for the next several hours; to avoid a skin rash, clean the area around the bandage with soap and water.
  3. If the needle site starts to bleed, apply pressure and raise your arm straight up for 5-10 minutes or until bleeding stops.
  4. If you experience dizziness or lightheadedness, stop what you're doing and sit down or lie down until you feel better; avoid performing any activity where fainting may lead to injury for at least 24 hours.
  5. Keep eating iron-rich foods.
  6. If you donate frequently, be sure to take multivitamins with iron to ensure you continue to replenish your iron stores before your next donation.
